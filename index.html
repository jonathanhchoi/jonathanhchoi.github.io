About

I am a researcher at the intersection of law and technology, focusing on empirical legal research and computational analysis of legal texts. My work explores how modern computational methods, including large language models and natural language processing, can enhance our understanding of legal systems and improve legal practice.

Based in New York, I combine expertise in programming languages like Python, R, and Stata with deep knowledge of legal theory and practice. My research has covered topics ranging from measuring clarity in legal text to studying the subjective costs of tax compliance.
